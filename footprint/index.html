footprint

"Walk the world, watch the fog lift."

An iOS travel tracker that paints a dark fog over the entire world map and "punches through" only where you've actually been — by GPS, photo EXIF, or imported tile data. Then layers cyan-blue admin-region highlights for countries, US states & counties, and Chinese 省/市/区县 on top. Tier-based achievements turn ordinary walks into a quiet game.

Problem Statement

Where you've actually been is invisible to your phone

Photo libraries hold the data, calendars hold the dates, but neither answers "what fraction of the world have I touched?" Existing fog-of-war apps are paid, dated, or locked to a single source — and none of them tell you that you've covered 17 of California's 58 counties.

🌍

Memory blurs by year

You can recall 2024's trips but not 2018's stopovers. There's no surface in iOS that says "this is the shape of everywhere you've ever been."

📷

Photos hold the data, hide it

20,000 photos with EXIF GPS, but no way to extrude them onto a map and see coverage. Photo libraries are timelines, not atlases.

🔒

Locked into legacy apps

Fog-of-World style apps own their tile format. Switch phones or apps and your years of walking dissolve. No graceful import path.

🏆

Coverage is uncountable

"Have I been to all 50 states?" needs a tally per admin region — countries, states, counties, 区县. Plain pin maps don't keep score.

User Personas

Three travelers, one fog

The app serves three distinct relationships with travel: the collector chasing coverage, the local explorer gamifying daily walks, and the photo-library archaeologist back-filling years of memory.

Primary · Coverage Collector

Frequent traveler

Has been to 30+ countries and wants a single beautiful surface that proves it — and one that flatly tells them they're missing two US states and four Chinese provinces.

  • See country & state totals at a glance
  • Drill down into US counties / 中国区县
  • Generate a 4:5 portrait poster to share
Secondary · Local Explorer

Urban walker

Doesn't fly often, but walks 5–15 km a day. Wants the fog peeling back tile-by-tile to reward "took a different route home" — exploration as quiet game.

  • Tile-level fog uncovered by GPS
  • Tier 1–5 achievements (50 → 25,000 tiles)
  • Smooth, jaggies-free zoom on city walks
Tertiary · Archive Importer

Photo archaeologist

Has years of geo-tagged photos and a tile export from another fog app. Wants the map to reflect everywhere they've been, not just the days the new app was installed.

  • Backfill from photo library EXIF
  • Import tile sets from prior apps
  • Three sources merge into one fog overlay
User Journey & Pain Points

From dark map to shared poster in four steps

The first install opens onto an almost-entirely-dark world — the fog is the canvas. Every step from there is about getting more light through it, and the app provides three independent paths to do so.

1

Open the fog

First launch: a black world with one bright punch where you stand. The contrast does the marketing — the user immediately wants more lit pixels.

Pain: cold-start map looks "empty"
2

Backfill history

Import photo EXIF locations and tile dumps from prior apps. Three independent sources merge into one fog index.

Pain: legacy app lock-in
3

Switch to Footprint

Tap the second tab: cyan-blue fills appear over visited countries / states / counties / 省 / 市 / 区县. The picker chips show which scope.

Pain: needs admin-region progress, not pins
4

Earn & share

Achievements unlock at 1 / 50 / 200 / 1k / 5k / 25k tiles and 1 / 10 / 25 / 50 / 100 countries. Generate a 4:5 poster and post it.

Pain: travel sharing is awkward without a polished export

Features & Capabilities

Six capabilities, one consistent visual language

The visual identity is borrowed directly from FootprintView: a single saturated cyan-blue (#45A5E8) for fills, a brighter cyan stroke (#99E0FF) for separation. One color across all admin levels keeps focus on "where" not "what zoom".

Performance

MKMapView culls off-screen overlays automatically — per-frame cost scales with viewport, not lifetime tile count. Soft-punch is one Core Image blur amortized over the app's lifetime; per-frame draw is a texture blit.

Visual fidelity

Two render modes split at slippy zoom 15.5: hard rects below (fast, jaggies invisible at ~5km screen width), soft sprite above (slow but smooth). Geometry tuned so the sprite's solid core covers diagonal neighbors at zoom 19.

Scaling to all countries

Curated paths for World/US/China; .other(ISO) falls through to a generic admin1 path backed by per-country Natural Earth shards. Adding a new country is a data drop — no code change.
